Shu Quan

I am a Master's student at Peking University, working with Prof. Yaodong Yang at the PKU-Alignment group, Institute for Artificial Intelligence. I also visited HKUST(GZ) NLP-LAB, advised by Prof. Xuming Hu.

I work on the reliability of LLM agents and reasoning systems in high-stakes real-world domains, including biomedicine, scientific literature, and system-level software. My research focuses on two interlocking questions: (1) how to diagnose failure modes that standard metrics miss, such as system-level, knowledge-level, and domain-specific reliability gaps; and (2) how to design supervision, evaluation, and observability methods that make these systems safer and more dependable under real-world constraints.

I am actively seeking PhD opportunities for Fall 2027. I'm always eager to connect with fellow researchers and explore potential collaborations or exchange ideas.

Medical knowledge & LLM alignment

Dense Clinical Contrasts Enhance Medical Knowledge Updating in Large Language Models

Yangmin Huang*, Shu Quan*, He Geng, Xin Ye, Qianyun Du, Zhiyang He, Jiaxue Hu, Xiaodong Tao

under review  ·  Co-first author (listed second)

We introduce SEER-Bench, a temporally anchored oncology-staging benchmark, and study how updated clinical knowledge should be structured as supervision. Under the same update budget, many-to-many EMQ supervision gives the strongest transfer and retention; Qwen3-4B reaches 64.8% on temporally anchored staging and outperforms four of seven external reference systems.

Agent & system reliability

A Blind Spot in Alignment: Quantifying Biosecurity Risks in Large Language Models

Shu Quan*, Tianfang Hao*, Sitong Fang, He Geng, Jiayi Zhou, Boyuan Chen, Kaile Wang, Donghai Hong, Juntao Dai, Yaodong Yang, Jiaming Ji

under review  ·  Co-first author (listed first, lead)

paper  /  code

We introduce SPIKE-Bench, a sequence-level biosecurity benchmark with 631 toxin-design prompts and a 3-stage evaluation funnel that measures compliance, biological plausibility, and predicted toxicity. Across 32 LLMs, functional harmfulness reaches 50.7% and is nearly uncorrelated with refusal rate; we also release BioSafe-Guard, reaching 98.9% detection at 0.3% FPR.

SafeCLI: Exploring System-Centric Risks in CLI Agents via Augmented Observability

Donghai Hong, Changhao Ju, Shu Quan, Yuyang Ren, Boyuan Chen, Jiayi Zhou, Kaile Wang, Juntao Dai, Yaodong Yang, Jiaming Ji

under review  ·  Co-author

paper  /  code

We propose OS-Monitor, a kernel-level plug-and-play detection toolkit with 28 detectors across 7 system-risk categories, and evaluate it on 314 Linux CLI tasks. We find that 74% of system-level harms produce no visible terminal signal; using OS-Monitor as an inference-time constraint improves average safety by 14.77%.

SLM-MATRIX: A Multi-Agent Trajectory Reasoning and Verification Framework for Enhancing Language Models in Materials Data Extraction

Xin Li*, Zhixuan Huang*, Shu Quan, Cheng Peng, Xiaoming Ma

npj Computational Materials, 2025  ·  SCI Q1  ·  Co-author

paper

We introduce SLM-MATRIX, a multi-agent framework combining MoA collaboration, MCTS-enhanced generator-discriminator reasoning, and dual cross-verification for structured data extraction from materials-science literature. It achieves 92.85% accuracy on BulkModulus and 77.68% on MatSynTriplet without model fine-tuning.

Analysis of the PM2.5-O3 Pollution Characteristics and Its Potential Sources in Major Cities in the Central Plains Urban Agglomeration from 2014 to 2020

Shu Quan, Miaohan Liu, Boxuan Chen, Yuehua Huang, Meijuan Wang, Qingxia Ma, Yan Han

Atmosphere, 2023, 14(1):92  ·  SCI  ·  First author

First-author study of PM2.5-O3 compound pollution in seven major cities of the Central Plains Urban Agglomeration from 2014 to 2020. We analyze spatiotemporal pollution patterns, meteorological drivers, and potential source transport, providing evidence for coordinated PM2.5-O3 pollution control.

Nonlinear Effects of Blue-Green Space Variables on Urban Cold Islands in Zhengzhou Analyzed with Random Forest Regression

Shu Quan*, Maojuan Li*, Tianqi Li, Haodong Liu, Yaohui Cui, Miaohan Liu

Frontiers in Ecology and Evolution, 2023  ·  SCI Q2  ·  Co-first author (listed first)

paper

We model nonlinear relationships between blue-green space configuration and urban cold-island effects using random forest regression, identifying blue-space proportion, area, and vegetation cover as key drivers, including planning-relevant thresholds around 140 m park width and 56% vegetation coverage.
